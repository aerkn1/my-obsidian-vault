### SECTION 4: Effectuation vs Causation

---

#### Q6: Not Effectuation
**Which of the following is NOT typical of effectual decision making?**

a) To start with resources and select among possible outcomes.
b) To calculate the downside potential and to risk no more than one can afford to lose.
c) To set up transactional relationships with customers and suppliers.
d) To see surprises and even failures as new opportunities.

**‚úÖ ANSWER: (c)**

**Why (c) is correct:**
- Effectuation uses **PARTNERSHIPS** (Crazy Quilt), NOT transactions
- **(a)** EFFECTUAL ‚úì - Bird-in-Hand (start with means/resources)
- **(b)** EFFECTUAL ‚úì - Affordable Loss principle
- **(c)** NOT EFFECTUAL ‚úó - Causation uses transactions; Effectuation uses partnerships
- **(d)** EFFECTUAL ‚úì - Lemonade principle (leverage surprises)

**Key**: Effectuation = **Partnerships**; Causation = **Transactions**

**Related**: [[04-Effectuation-vs-Causation]]

- **(b)** EFFECTUATION ‚úó - Means oriented (Bird-in-Hand)
